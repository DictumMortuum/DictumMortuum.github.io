There are many spells out there and they are too numerous for a person to sort out which ones are good and which ones are bad in every situation. Although people in the past have rated spells that are universally good (for instance Logic Ninja in his Batman wizard guide or Treadmonk in his guides to the schools of magic), I decided that doing the same thing and giving my own take would be completely dull and boring.
So, I was inspired to create the "Spell Effectiveness Calculator"; a simple program that will generate a score percentage - it will tell you how far your spell is from perfect. Keep in mind that this currently is a quick draft I made and while the front-end is pretty much done, many different things may change in the way that the score is calculated.
In order to utilize the calculator, simply fill in any relevant details about your spell and press the 'generate score' button. If you simply don't care about a specific category (for instance, if you don't care about the range or the area of effect of a spell), don't choose anything and the program will automatically exclude that section from the score. Also, two categories (Components and Allowed Defenses) feature checkboxes; all the checkboxes are weighted the same, except from the XP component and the Saving throw defense (they have a higher weight - again weights will be subject to change as the project matures). The 'clear selections' button is pretty straightforward - it will clear all the forms that you have filled in order to calculate the effectiveness of another spell without having to refresh the page.
As always, any comments are welcome.
